Protocols

How to do outcrossing?

  1. Cross mutant hermaphrodites with N2 males (ratio 1:3 to 1:5). Bojun picks 5 hermaphrodites and ~15 males.

  2. Cross heterozygous males from step #1 with N2 hermaphrodites at an increased males to hermaphrodites ratio (e. g. 5:1 to 7:1).

  3. Pick the F1 progeny (hermaphrodites) from step #2, and plate them individually (8-10 plates). Make sure that the male:hermaphrodite has a ration of approximately 1:1 in the plate from step #2.

  4. PCR for the plates from step #3 to check the presence of the mutation using a primer pair outside the deletion. Make sure to use both the mutant strain and N2 as controls.

  5. Use only one of the positive plate from step #4, and pick 12 worms from it, and plate the worms individually.

  6. PCR to check for homozygous mutants using plates of step 5 as templates using one primer inside and another outside the deletion. Make sure to use both the mutant strain and N2 as controls. By now, 2X outcrossing is completed.

  7. Repeat steps 1-6 to obtain 4X outcrossed mutant.

How to build a double mutant?

  1. Determine the chromosome locations of the two genes. If they are close (<10 map units) on the same chromosome, it may be necessary to use CRISPR to add a mutation to the single mutant.

  2. If one of the mutant gene is on X chromosome, cross N2 males with the mutant first.

  3. Use the males from step 2, which are homozygous mutant, to cross with the other mutant.

  4. If the male:hermaphrodite ratio of step 3 is approximately 1:1, plate 4-6 F1 progeny individually. If there are more hermaphrodites than males, plate more F1 individually. If neither of the mutant genes is on X chromosome, plate 10-12 progeny in this step.

  5. Do PCR as step 4 in the above-described procedure to check the presence of the first mutation.

  6. Pick ~20 worms from one of the positive plates in step 5, and plate them individually.

  7. PCR to identify plates from step 6 to identify homozygous worms for one of the two mutant genes (expected positive ¼ plates).

  8. For all the homozygous mutant plates of step 7, PCR to identify homozygous worms for the second gene (expected positive ¼ plates).

  9. If no homozygous mutant for the second gene is detected in step 8, do PCR to identify plates containing heterozygous mutation of the second gene, and pick 12 worms from the positive plate, and plate them individually.

  10. PCR to check homozygous mutation for the second gene.

How to do PCR?

  1. Dilute the 3X +K (proteinase K) buffer with water, and aliquote 5 or 6 µL per PCR tube.

  2. Place 20-30 worms into each PCR tube.

  3. Freeze the PCR tubes at -80 ℃ for >5 min, and thaw. Repeat this 1-2 times.

  4. Lyse the worms using the lysis program (65 ℃ for 1 hour followed by 95 ℃ for 15 min).

  5. Use 0.5 – 1 µL of the lysate as template to do PCR (35-40 cycles) in 15 µL volume.

  6. 50 µL formula for 3 samples with 16-17 µL volume. including (µL): Buffer 5, Mg2+ 4, dNTP 3.5 (2.5 mM), Taq 0.5, Primer 5 x 2, total 23 µL, and then add 27 µL H2O to 50 µL.

  7. Making gel. 100mL TAE solution add 1g agarose, after loading to gel, and then to run 20 to 25 mins.

  8. Load 6 µL ladder to first hole, and load wt, control, and other samples.

How to do sequence from Gel ?

  1. Add 250 µL QG buffer to Gel block cutted in 1 ml tube, and then heat them until to completely melt. centriguge (1 min 13000) to filter DNA to membrane.

  2. Add 500 µL PE buffer (with alcohol), centrifuge, repeat twice

  3. Add 15 µL EB buffer warmed up to 70 degree to filter tube center, keep at least 1 min, and then centrifuge once.

  4. Any oligo (2.5 µM) 10 µL + 5 µL DNA to pcr tube, and then order them in Quintarabio website. put printed sheet in small plastic bag, post to L3026.

  5. On the second day, download sequence result file, and then use SeqMan Pro (DNAstar) analyze *.ab1 file and genome DNA, find the 20-30 bp oligo before or after point mutation loci. heterozygous to show two peak. homozygous just to show one peak.

How to use PrimerSelect?

  1. Either Send Sequence to PrimerSelect or Enter Sequence. (Note: DNA sequence from sbd files may get truncated. Use seq files). You will now observe an “Untitled 1” window showing a schematic range of your DNA sequence.

  2. Go to Conditions, Primer Locations, and select Restrict Locations by Upper and Lower Primer Ranges. Define the range of your primer locations, and click OK. For the initial try, leave the “Avoid Locations Containing Repetitive Sequence within “10” bp of 3’, which is the default.

  3. Go to Locate, and PCR Primer Pairs.

  4. In the new graphic window of Located Primer Pairs), which shows various primer pairs (green and read arrow heads connected by dotted lines), click on one of them and notice that the bottom of the window displays alternative pairs as well.

  5. Right-click on the selected pair, and select Choose This Pair. Notice that the Untitled 1 graphic window now displays one pair of red and green lines with arrows. If you Choose a different pair, the corresponding pair of red and green lines will be displayed above those of the last pair.

  6. On the left side of the Untitled 1 graphic window, click on any of the three icons below the first one, you should now see the sequences of the upper and lower primers.

  7. Go to Report, Amplification Summary to see Optimal Annealing Temperature and Product Length.

How to make worm lysis solution?

  1. Proteinase K (BP1700-100) to make 10ml stock solution should be easy for next time. Fisher BioReagents™ Proteinase K (Tritirachium album/Molecular Biology) order from fisher scientific.

  2. 67 µg/ml (work concentration) ➔ 200 µg/ml (3 × stock concentration); 10 mg/ml (stock solution store in -20 °C freezer);

  3. Making 3 x stock solution: 20µl (stock solution, 10 mg/ml) add ➔ 1 ml 3 × PCR buffer (330 µL: 10 x Taq reaction buffer + 650 µL: H2O + 20 µL: stock Proteinase K 10 mg/ml solution).

  4. Dissolve the Proteinase K:
    Method 1 : Dissolve in 10 mM Tris HCl, pH 7.5, 20 mM calcium chloride, and 50% glycerol, then store at –20°C.
    Method 2 : Dissolve in 50 mM Tris HCl (pH 8.0), 1–5 mM calcium acetate, then store at 2°C–8°C.

Making Electrocompetent E. coli (Dh30B) Cells (large batch)

Material Preparation
  • ~50ml LB for starter culture in a 250ml flask.

  • 300 ml of fresh LB medium in a 1000 ml flask (2 flasks).

  • 800ml 10% glycerol in a 1000ml bottle (2 bottles).

  • 0.5 ml tubes (~300 tubes).

  • Autoclave all and put in cold room a day before making electrocompetent cells.
Procedure
  1. Grow an overnight culture of Dh30B strain in ~50ml LB medium.

  2. Check the OD at 600nm (expected stationary phase OD600 = >1.0).

  3. Inoculate 300 ml of LB medium in a 1000 ml flask (300ml x 2 flasks) with overnight, stationary-phase culture to an OD600 of ~0.05. For example: if OD600 of stationary phase culture is 1.5 then 0.05 (OD600) X 300ml = 1.5(OD600) X ?ml which is 10ml stationary phase culture required to make 300ml LB OD600 to 0.05.

  4. Grow the cells for approximately 2-3 hours, until they reach mid-exponential phase. In general, this is an OD600 of 0.4-0.6.

  5. ~30 min before you plan to prepare your cells, set the centrifuge to 4°C. Chill the subculture in ice for ~20 minutes before centrifuging.

  6. Transfer the cells from first flask to 6x 50 ml Falcon conical tubes.

  7. Pellet the cells by centrifugation for 5 minutes at 6,000 RPM. Remove promptly and pour off supernatant. Transfer the cells from second flask to these 6x 50ml Falcon conical tubes, then resuspend the pellet by pipetting, centrifuge again (5 minutes at 6,000 RPM) and pour off supernatant.

  8. Wash by adding 40 ml of chilled 10% glycerol to each tube, then resuspend the pellet by pipetting. Centrifuge for 4 minutes at 6,000 RPM. Remove promptly and pour off supernatant. Repeat for at least five wash cycles in chilled 10% glycerol (pellet will be loose after each wash).Before final wash combine 6 pellets into two 50 ml Falcon tubes.

  9. Resuspend each pellet in approximately 5 ml of 10% glycerol. (2 pellets x 5ml= ~12ml).

  10. Divide into ~50 µl aliquots in 0.5 ml tubes. Freeze promptly at -80°C.

How to make male worm using heat shock ?

  1. Getting males from a hermaphrodite population. This is a modified version of protocol originally written by Michael Koelle at Yale University.

  2. Materials and Reagents including NGM plates and 25 and 30 °C incubators.

  3. Set up ~6 NGM plates (seeded with normal bacterial food E. coli OP50-1) with 10 L4 hermaphrodites each.

  4. Heat shock 4-6 h (no more than 6 h) at 30 °C.

  5. Move plates to 25 °C. You should get a few males per plate in the F1 generation.
    Note: To get more males, it is best to set them up with an excess of L4 hermaphrodites to ensure more males recover in the next generation.

  6. Male stocks should be maintained by selecting out an excess of males, along with a few L4 hermaphrodites in each generation (usually, male to hermaphrodite ratio is about 1: 10).

Oocyte experimental tips

  1. The injecting needle size: 4 division at high mag, greater than 5 will kill oocyte. 4 is the best size for injection. At high mag 2.8 µm /division. Actually, its size should be close to 11 µm. use niu's #65 puller protocol to make the injecting pipette.
  2. 2.5 μl cRNA will inject at least 30 occytes.
  3. Always to make the new pipette to record voltage and current in case of some pipettes were blocked, and polished (ethonal lamp) the pipette backside in case of destroying the chlorided silver wire.
  4. Clearly write the note, especially the cRNA injecting time and oocyte recording time. in order to grasp cRNA expression speed.

How to make worm plates ?

  1. 4L volume consists of Cholesterol* 4ml, NaCl 12g, peptone 10g, bactoagar 68g. * Cholesterol stock solution is 5mg/ml in EtOh;

  2. Add 4L ddH2O to the 4L jar, place the jar on magnetic stirrer;

  3. Seal the jar mouth, autoclave for about 1h 40m (program: liquid 6);

  4. During the autoclave, turn on the water bath;

  5. After autoclave finished, move jar to water bath for 1h, let solution cooled down to about 55 °C;

  6. Add 4ml 1 M CaCl2, 4ml 1M MgSO4, 100ml 1M KH2PO4;

  7. Prepare about 440 60mm-dishes; Fix the top end of the tube to the pump; step on the pedal to fill each dish with 10ml of solution;

  8. Wash the tube with hot water, put it back to the beaker with the top end at the top. autoclave each time;

  9. The second day, prepare bacteria layer: take 2 sterile 50ml centrifuge tubes; burn an alcohol lamp;

  10. Add 50ml LB medium (without Amp) to 2 tubes, and to incubate them 37 °C for 12h or shake them;

  11. Drop 2 - 3 drops to worm plate to form an round area roughly 1 - 2 cm diameter;

  12. When plates completely dried, updown to keep 4 °C room.
